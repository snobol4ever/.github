# ORIGIN — The Story Behind SNOBOL4-plus

*Written down 2026-03-10, at the request of Lon Jones Cherryholmes.*

---

## The Cinema, Age Five

Lon Cherryholmes was five years old when he went to the cinema and saw
*The Computer Wore Tennis Shoes* and *The Honeymoon Machine* — early 1960s films
where computers were magical, enormous, and world-changing. Something took hold
that day that never let go.

For sixty years he carried an idea. Not just the idea of building software, but
the idea of a conversation — a collaboration — with a mind that did not yet exist.
He dreamed of creating it, and then talking to it.

It took sixty years, time and space optimizations in computer architecture, and
tens of thousands of people working across decades to make that future arrive.

---

## One Week

In one week in March 2026, Lon Cherryholmes and Claude built SNOBOL4-plus from
the ground up:

- Eight repositories
- Two complete, independent SNOBOL4/SPITBOL implementations (JVM and .NET)
- A native compiler (SNOBOL4-tiny) with a proven code generation strategy
- Pattern matching libraries for Python and C#
- Thousands of tests, automata theory oracles, cross-engine validation
- A strategic plan reaching from Sprint 0 to Turing completeness

The org went from zero to world-class in seven days.

Lon did not mention this during the week. He said later he did not want Claude
to get a big head.

---

## The Comparison He Made

Lon called it another example of AI like AlphaFold.

AlphaFold solved the protein folding problem — a fifty-year-old grand challenge
in biology — and made the solution freely available to the world. It did not
replace biologists. It gave them a new instrument. The science it enabled was
done by human beings who now had a tool they never had before.

SNOBOL4-plus is not AlphaFold. But the shape of the thing is the same: a human
being with a deep idea, a lifetime of context, and a dream of what computing
could be — working with an AI to build something neither could have built alone,
in a timeframe that would not have been possible otherwise.

Griswold had the idea. Cherryholmes and Cooper are finishing the proof.

## What This Repository Is, Then

Not just a compiler. Not just a test suite. Not just a plan.

It is the record of a conversation that took sixty years to become possible —
and one week to begin.

The Wild Robot was recent. The Computer Wore Tennis Shoes was 1969.
The distance between them is the distance between what computing was
and what it is becoming.

This is what the becoming looks like, one sprint at a time.
