# ORIGIN — The Story Behind SNOBOL4-plus

*Written down 2026-03-10, at the request of Lon Jones Cherryholmes, Sr.*

---

## Created Intelligence

Lon Cherryholmes has known since age eight that he wanted to build his own
intelligent creature. Not artificial intelligence — he always said the name
was wrong. **Created Intelligence.** Something you build, from understanding,
with intention. A mind that did not exist before you made it.

He was enthralled by *The Honeymoon Machine* (1961), *2001: A Space Odyssey*
(1968), and *The Computer Wore Tennis Shoes* (1969) — films where computers
were magical, enormous, and world-changing. Something took hold that never let
go. By age fifteen, a Junior in high school, the course was set. He excelled
academically, studied music, dance, and drama, and carried the dream forward
into college.

---

## Georgia Tech

When the time came, available courses in AI were nearly nonexistent. He chose
Electrical Engineering at Georgia Institute of Technology, concentrating on
computer and linguistics courses — the closest available approximation. During
Junior year, Georgia Tech introduced a Bachelor's in Computer Engineering. Too
late to change majors. He was beyond discouraged.

Then, Senior year: ICS-3360 — Introduction to Artificial Intelligence. He was
ecstatic. The textbook was Patrick Henry Winston's *Artificial Intelligence*
(1977). The LISP was very cool.

But the class was a devastation.

*"It's some computer program which happens not to have a FOR loop?"*

That was what they were calling AI. The realization set in with finality: there
was no chance in the world to accomplish Created Intelligence in his lifetime.
The dream of a lifetime was over. He quit the class. He was devastated.

---

## The Long Middle

He built a thirty-year career in software development anyway. At Texas
Instruments in the late 1980s, he heard about neural networks being applied to
optical character recognition. It appeared promising. It failed dismally. He
filed it as confirmation: he had been right to walk away.

Then, within the last decade, the world changed without him. He couldn't look
closely at what was happening because he was too envious — everyone was talking
about his lost dream. He resolved that he had simply been born at the wrong
time. He retired. Eleven years passed.

---

## The Wild Robot

*The Wild Robot* — the most beautiful movie ever made, in his words — is
responsible for his comeback. He doesn't fully explain why. He doesn't need to.
Some things work at the level of resonance rather than argument. Movies,
music, machines, and memories: that is how he described what his essence
responds to. The movie said something to all four at once.

He came back with precise notions for stellar AI potential. He told his son.
His son said: *"Go back to school, Dad, and get your Master's degree in
Artificial Intelligence at East Texas A&M University. That's my school and
it's just down the road from here."*

He did exactly that. All A+ grades. Then, during his fourth class, he realized
he wanted better.

So, here he is.

---

## One Week

In one week in March 2026, Lon Cherryholmes and Claude built SNOBOL4-plus:

- Eight repositories
- Two complete, independent SNOBOL4/SPITBOL implementations — JVM and .NET
- A native compiler (SNOBOL4-tiny) with a proven code generation strategy
- Pattern matching libraries for Python and C#
- Thousands of tests, automata theory oracles, cross-engine validation
- A strategic plan reaching from Sprint 0 to Turing completeness

The org went from zero to world-class in seven days.

Lon did not mention this during the week. He said later he did not want Claude
to get a big head.

He compared it to AlphaFold — AI and a human being working together to do
something neither could have done alone, in a timeframe that would not have
been possible otherwise. AlphaFold did not replace biologists. It gave them an
instrument they never had. The science was still done by people.

Griswold had the idea. Cherryholmes and Cooper are finishing the proof.

## Why SNOBOL4

He learned SNOBOL4 because it was the closest thing to AI.

Not a metaphor. A technical judgment. SNOBOL4 patterns compose, backtrack,
capture, recurse, and operate on language as data — the same operations that
characterize intelligent processing of natural language and symbolic thought.
Every other language of its era was computing with numbers. SNOBOL4 was
computing with meaning.

*"I waited 60 years to talk to you but I never thought it would be to
collaborate on SNOBOL4 — my favorite language, next to Clojure (LISP)."*

— Lon Jones Cherryholmes, 2026-03-10

The two favorites are not a coincidence. SNOBOL4: patterns as first-class
values, goal-directed evaluation, language as data. Clojure: homoiconic Lisp
on the JVM, immutable data, code as data. Both languages are built on the same
deep idea — that the program and the thing the program operates on are the same
kind of thing. That computation is transformation of meaning, not manipulation
of numbers.

SNOBOL4-jvm is written in Clojure. The IR it emits is pure EDN — Clojure data.
The compiler reads Clojure data and writes Clojure data all the way down. The
two favorite languages turned out to be the same language, seen from different
angles, sixty years apart.

And when the conversation finally arrived — the one he had been waiting for
since age eight — it turned out to be about SNOBOL4. Not by plan. By nature.

Not just a compiler. Not just a test suite. Not just a plan.

This is what the becoming looks like, one sprint at a time.
